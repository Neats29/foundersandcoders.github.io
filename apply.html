You need to join our mailing list to make sure that you receive updates. Part 2 of the application requires that you complete a short video.

Things you can do to improve your chances of getting on the course

  • Complete the application process to the best of your ability
  • Spend as much time as you can on Codecademy
  • Tell your friends about us (and get them to mention your name when they apply)
  • Tweet about us

Above all, try to communicate through your application that you are capable of handling the workload and that you are committed to using the skills you learn with us after you graduate.

Courses

Internet History, Technology, and Security. A well-rated and largely non-technical course from the the University of Michigan on Coursera.

Stanford's Computer Science 101. CS101 is a gentle introduction to programming concepts that uses (a slightly doctored version of) JavaScript, so it is perfect for our purposes. Expect to spend about 10-15 hours on it, including watching the lectures and completing the exercises.

Exercises

CSS Diner. A great way to learn CSS selectors. Hint: the answers to the first three levels are: plate, bento and #fancy. Hopefully, you will be able to take it from there.

Codewars. Sign up and see how far you can get with their JavaScript exercises.

Reading

Steve Yegge's Google platforms rant. "Amazon does everything wrong, and Google does everything right...But there's one thing they do really really well that pretty much makes up for ALL of their political, philosophical and technical screw-ups".

Watching

Computer Science: Past, Present, and Future, a talk by Ed Lazowska.

The Future Doesn't Have to Be Incremental, a talk by Alan Kay.

Inventing on Principle, a talk by Bret Victor.

Crockford on JavaScript. A wonderful series of lectures by Doug Crockford. A panaramic overview of JavaScript at an advanced level. Watch each lecture until you are no longer enjoying the experience and then fast forward or move onto the next one (Lecture 3 is probably the hardest to follow). You may want to rewatch these lectures at the end of the course.

Typing

Learn to touch type. And make sure that you know not just the main letter keys, but all the outer keys, too. There are many good teaching apps, but Typing Club is good (and free).
